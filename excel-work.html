Project Name

CIBC Branch Analytics: Understanding Product Uptake and Customer Value

Overview

As part of a simulated CIBC analytics team, I analyzed product signups and customer value across Ontario branches. The goal was to generate insights to support targeted outreach, cross-selling, and branch performance improvements.

Objective

  • Identify high- and low-performing branches.
  • Understand where high-value customers are concentrated.
  • Recommend where cross-selling may be most effective.
  • Conditional formatting and slicers to highlight outliers and enable exploratory filtering

    • Methodology

    Excel Features Demonstrated

    To build this tool, I used a combination of Excel’s core analytical functions:

    • VLOOKUP to assign a risk score based on number of late payments
    • SUMIF/SUMIFS to aggregate payments by city and business type
    • A calculated column to determine each customer’s delinquency rate, defined as late payments divided by total credit accounts
    • Conditional formatting and slicers to highlight outliers and enable exploratory filtering

    Results

    • Cities like Hamilton and London showed higher rates of delinquent accounts, suggesting localized risk
    • Customers with more than three late payments often had delinquency rates above 100%, flagging them as urgent cases
    • A 0–5 risk score system, generated using formulas, streamlined account triage and supported faster credit review workflows

    Business Relevance

    This dashboard demonstrates how Excel can be used to automate credit risk classification and identify high-risk segments within a portfolio. It reflects how financial institutions like CIBC might leverage spreadsheet-based models for internal monitoring, especially in small-to-mid scale portfolios or early-stage evaluations.
