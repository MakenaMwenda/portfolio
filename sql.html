Project Name

Client Product Engagement Insights – Simulated SQL Case Study for RBC

Overview

This project simulates the kind of task a Data Analyst Intern might complete for RBC’s Digital Banking & Analytics team. Using dummy client-product data in SQLite and DBeaver, I analyzed product engagement across Canadian provinces and client age groups. The case study demonstrates how SQL can support business questions such as identifying upsell candidates, evaluating product preferences by segment, and uncovering regional trends. Key techniques included joins, aggregations, filtering, and conditional logic—skills aligned with real-world analytics workflows.

Objective

The goal was to showcase practical SQL capabilities for business analysis. These include aggregating product usage across client segments, identifying marketing opportunities by age and region, and supporting business questions with data-driven insights.

Methodology

SQL Features Demonstrated

To explore the data, I applied the following SQL techniques:

  • JOINs to connect client, product, and transaction records
  • COUNT(), AVG(), ROUND() to measure engagement and financial patterns
  • WHERE and HAVING clauses for filtering based on conditions
  • GROUP BY to organize insights by region or client demographics
  • ORDER BY to rank results and identify high- or low-performing segments

Sample Queries and Insights

Query 1: Average Product Usage by Province

Calculated the average number of products held per client in each province.

Finding: Manitoba leads in average product usage, followed by Alberta and British Columbia.

Query 2: Top 3 Products Among Clients Aged 25–34

Segmented data by age to find the most commonly held products among younger clients.

Finding: Credit cards were the most popular, followed by savings accounts and investment products.

Query 3: Average Balance by Product Type

Analyzed how average balances varied across product types.

Finding: Investments and savings accounts had the highest average balances.

Query 4: Identify Clients with Only One Product (Upsell Candidates)

Flagged under-engaged clients who hold only one RBC product.

Finding: Several clients showed untapped growth potential.

Results

  • Stronger product engagement in Manitoba and Alberta
  • Clients aged 25–34 preferred credit and investment products
  • High average balances in savings and investment accounts
  • One-product clients identified for targeted upsell campaigns

Business Relevance

These insights simulate how RBC could leverage product usage data to drive cross-sell strategy, personalize outreach, and enhance client retention. The case study reflects applied SQL proficiency aligned with the goals of a Digital Banking & Analytics team.
