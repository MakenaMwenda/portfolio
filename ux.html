Project Name

Varsity Newspaper – Analysing Pickup Rates Using Tableau

Project Description

As Creative Director at The Varsity, the University of Toronto’s student newspaper, I led visual strategy, layout design, and brand consistency across print and digital formats. While in this role, I became increasingly interested in how data could be used to optimize our content reach. This led to the development of a dashboard that visualizes newspaper pickup patterns across distribution stands in Toronto.

Objective

  • Understand pickup behavior across different days, locations, and weather conditions.
  • Identify which stands and program zones had the highest engagement.
  • Optimize print volumes and distribution schedules to reduce waste and improve circulation efficiency.

Methodology

Data Preparation

Over a period of 4 months, I tracked newspaper pickup data across 10 physical stands placed in various zones across campus. Each stand’s inventory was restocked daily, and pickup counts were recorded once per day using physical sheet tracking and manual entry. This provided a consistent daily time series for each stand, allowing for comparative analysis across locations, days of the week, and program zones.

Feature Selection Rationale

I selected variables such as day of the week, program zone, and stand location based on hypotheses about factors likely to influence pickup behavior:

  • Day of the Week: Midweek editions (e.g., Wednesday and Thursday) might see higher engagement due to routine campus schedules and more stable foot traffic.
  • Program Zone: I wanted to explore whether faculty demographics or student routines influenced pickup rates.
  • Stand Location: I expected location type (social areas vs. academic halls) to significantly impact pickup volume.
  • Weather: I introduced daily weather (rain vs. clear) to test if it explained sudden drops in pickup rates.

These engineered variables helped surface actionable insights, such as adjusting print volume on rainy days or optimizing stand placement.

Dashboard Development

  • A map of distribution stands using latitude and longitude.
  • Bar and line graphs analyzing pickup trends by day of the week, weather, and time of drop.
  • Dual-axis charts comparing pickup percentage with count data per stand.

Results

  • Midweek drops, particularly Thursdays, saw the highest pickup rates, indicating alignment with academic routines.
  • Hart House and Koffler Centre emerged as top-performing stands, with strong readership in Mixed and Social Sciences zones.
  • Weather impacted behavior—clear days boosted pickups while rain/snow reduced them, supporting seasonal strategy changes.
  • Time of drop had minimal influence, offering operational flexibility.

Outcome & Impact

As a result of this analysis, I presented data-driven recommendations to the editorial and distribution teams. These led to several improvements:

  • 15–20% reduction in leftover print waste on low-pickup days by adjusting print volume.
  • Two stands repositioned to higher-traffic zones, increasing pickups by ~12%.
  • Midweek content planning was improved, with features and opinion columns timed to align with highest engagement.
  • Weather-aware distribution allowed teams to reduce waste on poor weather days based on past trends.

These changes improved reach, reduced waste, and provided a framework for ongoing distribution performance monitoring through Tableau.
